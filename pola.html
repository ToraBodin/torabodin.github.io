POLA

POLA is a tangible artefact that draws inspiration from vintage polaroid photos, online photo storage and the slow design philosophy.

The project was initially created during a group project at Chalmers University of Technology. I am currently continuing the work with the next iteration of the prototype, with the aim of creating a viable research product.

Context: Course, tangible interaction, group project with Daniel Duvanå, Julia Wallén and Yibin Huai.

My role: Concept, Research, Prototyping, Programming, Evaluation

Challenge

The initial challenge for Pola was to design “An object that will repeatedly break down and be in need of repair”. It should be a tangible artefact, and the common consensus of the group was to design something that would fit into the framework of speculative design.

Ideation and explorative research

Things we repair = Things we care about Things we care about = Expensive or nostalgic Nostalgic things in home environment = notebooks, childhood memories, photographs

Photographs fall down and needs to be put back up.

This is a reflective moment that makes you look at things that usually fade into the background.

Resurface faded memories by making them “break” and require interaction to bring them back.

The interaction becomes a polaroid “shake”.
